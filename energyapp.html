Project Energy App

The Energy App is a modern, accessible mobile solution for managing household energy and water services. It lets users visualize consumption data, record meter readings (manually or via scan), manage tariffs, and view bills—all within an intuitive interface designed to meet both user needs and business goals.

The Design Process

In designing the Energy App, I followed a four-phase process: Research (stakeholder workshops, interviews, usability tests and a WCAG AA audit) to surface pain points and accessibility needs; Ideation (user flows and low-fidelity Figma wireframes) refined with stakeholder feedback; UX/UI Design & Prototyping (high-fidelity, brand-aligned screens, interactive prototypes and documented components); and Testing & Refinement (usability sessions, data-visualization tweaks and micro-interaction polish). Throughout, close collaboration with developers in Jira and Confluence ensured feasibility and a smooth handoff.

Project Highlights

  • Interactive Consumption Charts: Clear, real-time visualizations of energy and water usage
  • Meter-Reading Workflow: Manual entry and scan-based capture, plus a history log
  • Tariff & Billing Management: Easy tariff adjustments and bill overview
  • Accessibility (WCAG AA): Scalable typography, high-contrast colors, and inclusive design
  • OCross-Functional Collaboration: Ongoing coordination with stakeholders and developers via Jira & Confluence

Conclusion

The Energy App delivers a user-centric, accessible platform for energy and water management. Key features—interactive consumption charts, meter-reading history, and tariff controls—offer tangible user value. This project strengthened my UX/UI design skills, honed my user-testing expertise, and underscored the importance of close collaboration across disciplines.
